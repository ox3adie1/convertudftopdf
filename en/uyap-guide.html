UYAP Guide: Understanding Turkey's Court Document System

Last updated: April 13, 2026 · 9 min read

If you are an international lawyer, researcher, or expat dealing with the Turkish legal system, you have likely encountered documents in unfamiliar formats, references to a system called "UYAP," or files with a .udf extension that refuse to open on your computer. This comprehensive guide explains everything you need to know about Turkey's digital judiciary system, the UDF file format, and how to work with Turkish court documents.

What is UYAP?

UYAP stands for Ulusal Yargi Agi Platformu, which translates to National Judiciary Informatics System. It is Turkey's comprehensive digital judiciary platform, developed and maintained by the Ministry of Justice. UYAP is one of the most extensive e-justice systems in the world, handling virtually all judicial processes in Turkey electronically.

Launched in the early 2000s and continuously expanded since then, UYAP has fundamentally transformed how the Turkish judiciary operates. What was once a paper-based system with physical case files and in-person filings has become an almost entirely digital ecosystem serving over 200,000 legal professionals and connecting more than 1,000 judicial institutions.

Key fact: As of 2026, virtually all court proceedings in Turkey are managed through UYAP. Every petition, court decision, hearing minute, indictment, notification, and expert report passes through this system.

UYAP's Core Components

The UYAP system consists of several interconnected components, each serving a specific user group within the Turkish legal ecosystem:

1. Case Management System

At its core, UYAP is a comprehensive case management platform. Every case filed in Turkish courts - from small claims to Constitutional Court proceedings - is registered, tracked, and managed within UYAP. The system handles case numbering and registration, assignment of cases to judges and courts, scheduling of hearings and deadlines, tracking of case status and progress, and archiving of completed cases.

2. UYAP Document Editor

The UYAP Document Editor is a proprietary word processing application used to create all legal documents within the system. It functions similarly to Microsoft Word but is specifically designed for the Turkish judiciary. Documents created with this editor are saved in the UDF (UYAP Document Format) - a proprietary file format that can only be opened by the editor itself.

The editor provides rich text formatting capabilities including bold, italic, underline, font customization, table creation (essential for legal documents), pre-built templates for common document types such as petitions, decisions, and hearing minutes, electronic signature integration, and automatic case reference numbering.

3. Lawyer Portal (Avukat Portal)

The UYAP Lawyer Portal is a web-based interface that allows licensed Turkish lawyers to interact with the court system remotely. Through this portal, lawyers can file petitions and motions electronically, track the status of their cases, receive court notifications and decisions, access case documents and download UDF files, communicate with courts, and pay court fees electronically. Access requires Turkish bar association membership and either an electronic signature (e-imza) or mobile signature (mobil imza).

4. Citizen Portal (Vatandas Portal)

Turkish citizens can access basic case information through the e-Government portal (e-Devlet). This provides case status inquiries, court date information, basic document access, and court fee payment. Unlike the lawyer portal, citizen access is more limited and read-only.

5. SEGBIS (Video Hearing System)

SEGBIS is UYAP's integrated video conferencing system for remote court hearings. Witnesses, defendants, or parties can participate in hearings from different locations, including other courthouses, prisons, or consulates abroad. This component has become increasingly important for cross-border legal proceedings.

Understanding UDF Files

The UDF (UYAP Document Format) is the proprietary file format at the heart of UYAP's document management system. For anyone outside the UYAP network, these files present a significant accessibility challenge. For a more detailed technical overview, see our What is UDF? guide.

Technical Characteristics of UDF

  • File extension: .udf
  • Internal structure: ZIP-compressed archive containing document data
  • Content format: Rich text with full formatting support
  • Character encoding: Full Turkish character support (including special characters such as ş, ğ, ı, o, u, c)
  • Table support: Complex table structures common in legal documents
  • Template system: Pre-built templates for various legal document types
  • Compatibility: Only readable by the UYAP Document Editor

Why UDF and Not PDF or DOCX?

A common question from international legal professionals is why Turkey uses a proprietary format instead of universal standards like PDF or DOCX. Several factors explain this decision. First, control and security: a proprietary format gives the Ministry of Justice full control over document creation, editing, and distribution. Documents created in UDF are tightly integrated with UYAP's authentication and electronic signature systems. Second, workflow integration: UDF files are deeply integrated with UYAP's case management workflow. They carry metadata that links them to specific cases, parties, and judicial actions. Third, historical reasons: UYAP was developed in the early 2000s when the current landscape of open document formats was less mature. The system was built with its own document format as part of its comprehensive design.

If you are an international lawyer involved in cross-border cases with Turkey, understanding UYAP and UDF is essential. Here is what you need to know for practical purposes:

Receiving Documents from Turkish Counsel

When your Turkish counterpart sends you court documents, they may arrive as UDF files attached to emails or shared through file transfer. These files cannot be opened with any standard software on your computer. Convert them to PDF using convertudftopdf.com to read and archive them.

Working with Turkish Co-Counsel

Your Turkish co-counsel has access to the UYAP Lawyer Portal and can retrieve case documents, file petitions, and manage proceedings. Request that they provide documents in PDF format when possible. When UDF files are unavoidable, use our converter to access the content.

Document Authentication and Apostille

For documents that need to be used in foreign proceedings, Turkish court documents typically require apostille certification under the Hague Convention. The electronic signatures embedded in UDF files are legally valid within Turkey but may need additional certification for international use. Work with your Turkish counsel to obtain properly apostilled copies.

Translation Considerations

UYAP by the Numbers

To understand the scale of the UYAP system, consider these figures:

  • 200,000+ active users including judges, prosecutors, lawyers, and court staff
  • 85+ million citizens with access through the e-Government portal
  • 1,000+ connected courthouses, prosecutors' offices, and judicial institutions
  • 20+ years of operation and continuous development since the early 2000s
  • Millions of case files managed electronically each year
  • All 81 provinces of Turkey connected to the system

UYAP's International Recognition

UYAP has received international recognition as one of the most comprehensive e-justice systems globally. It has been studied and referenced by various international organizations including the Council of Europe's European Commission for the Efficiency of Justice (CEPEJ), the World Bank's governance programs, and the European Union's e-justice initiatives. Several countries have consulted with Turkey when developing their own judicial informatics systems.

Key Turkish Legal Terms
